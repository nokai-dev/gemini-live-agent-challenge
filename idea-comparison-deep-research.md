### 3. ACADEMIC RESEARCH FINDINGS

**From PubMed & Medical Journals:**

**"Development of Communication Assistive Technology for Persons with Deaf-Blindness" (2025):**
- "There are currently **no best practice training methods** with communication assistive devices for individuals with deafblindness"
- Current solutions: Braille display notetaker + iPhone (expensive, clunky)

**"Beyond the Fingertips: Imagining Haptic Technologies for a Deafblind Future" (BMJ Medical Humanities, 2024):**
- "Haptics are already in use... **but these are not catch-all solutions**"
- "The sense of touch can be impaired in older people"
- "Not everyone has learnt tactile sign languages or braille"
- **Gap identified:** Need for non-tactile, AI-powered solutions

**National Center on Deafblindness (2025):**
- Only **7% of disabled respondents** feel represented in AI development
- Deafblind individuals rely on: braille displays, screen readers, specialized communication devices
- **No mention of AI-powered real-time multimodal solutions**

| Solution Type | Examples | Limitations | Price |

| **Deaf Apps** | Ava, Rogervoice | Speech-to-text only | $10-30/mo |
| **Blind Apps** | Be My Eyes, Aira | Human assistance, not AI | Free-$30/mo |

**The Gap:** No solution combines **real-time vision + voice + interruption** for deafblind users. EchoBridge would be the first.
