# Phase 2: Mandatory Critique Results

## Scoring Legend
- **0-3**: Poor - Major flaws, unlikely to win

## Idea 1: SightSync - AI Vision Assistant for the Blind

### Originality Critic: 9/10
**Verdict**: Highly memorable and emotionally resonant
- **Strengths**: Taps into universal empathy for accessibility. "Be My Eyes but AI" is instantly understandable. Judges will remember the demo of helping a blind person navigate.
- **Concerns**: Be My Eyes already exists with AI features. Need clear differentiation.
- **Recommendation**: Emphasize real-time conversational aspect vs. static image analysis.

### Judging Criteria Critic: 8/10
**Verdict**: Strong rubric alignment
- **Innovation (40%)**: Excellent - Real-time multimodal (voice + vision) with interruption handling
- **Technical (30%)**: Strong - Uses Gemini Live API, ADK, requires robust error handling
- **Demo (30%)**: Excellent - Highly visual, emotional, clear problem/solution
- **Concerns**: Need to prove it's better than existing solutions.

### Market Viability Critic: 9/10
**Verdict**: Massive market, clear value
- **Market**: 253M visually impaired people globally
- **Business Model**: Freemium, B2B (institutions), partnerships with accessibility orgs
- **Future Potential**: Expand to elderly, low-vision, situational blindness (driving)
- **Judge Appeal**: High - Social impact + commercial viability

### Disruptive Critic: 7/10
**Verdict**: Strong but somewhat expected
- **Blind Spot**: Everyone thinks of blind assistance for vision AI. What's the twist?
- **Missing Element**: Could combine with navigation (GPS), social (recognizing emotions), or work (reading documents)
- **Suggestion**: Add unique angle like "emotional tone detection" or "social navigation"

**AVERAGE: 8.25/10** ✅ PASSES
